#### Commands, Arguments, and Options
*Shell command line* are composed of one or more words, seperated by spaces or tabs. 

**Syntax** first word on the line is the command, anyrhing after are *arguments* also reffered to as *paramenters*

> Example
> 
> `lp myfile`

*lp* is the command and the arguemt is the name of a file *myfile*

**options** gives commands information on what it is going to do

>Example
>
> `lp -h myfile`

the *-h* tells lp not to print the abnner page

sometome options have own argumetns such as specifying destination printer *lp1* in `lp -d lp1 -h myfile`

## Files

>files are the most important typ of "things" on any unix system

**Three Important Files**
- **Regular files** - Text files contain readable charactors
- **Executable** - AKA programs, invoked as commands (the shell it self is an execxutable)

- **Directories** - Like folders, can contain files or other directories
